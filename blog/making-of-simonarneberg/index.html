The Making of simonarneberg.com

January 2026

Well, Here We Are

If you're reading this (which you are), then you already have a pretty good indication of what this website is like. I created simonarneberg.com in my free time after attending a Claude Code workshop, and I was so intrigued by the world of possibilities it opened that I had to try my hand at making a personal website with it. I don't easily fall into the hype surrounding generative AI tools, but Claude honestly feels like one of the most game-changing tools I've worked with in a long time. As the barrier to entry got lower and lower, I figured I might as well give it a go and create my own website!

Overall Vision

As I wrote on the landing page, I intend this site to be a space for me to share my ideas with the world, and a chance to grow my technical and creative skills in the process. I'm sure it will change, but I would like to keep this site somewhat up-to-date with the latest developments in my life, both professional and personal. Some people would say to avoid mixing those worlds, but I try to live an integrated life where I can be my full self across a variety of contexts.

Working With Claude

In my experience, Claude needs a good amount of guidance in order to get the outputs you want. For this site, I constantly iterated on every tiny detail, from the color/texture of the background to the exact angle of rotation upon hovering over the navigation bar "home" icon (6 degrees, with 1.1x scale-up, if you're curious). When I think of an addition, I generally try to describe it in as clear language as possible, adding as much context as I can. Only then, after dumping my brain out, do I prompt Claude to prompt me with any questions or clarifications needed before creating and executing the actual implementation plan. And then I iterate until I'm satisfied. Oftentimes this reveals some detail which I failed to account for previously, but with which Claude may have jumped to some assumption without specific prompting to ask.

But enough about Claude!

Across the site, all textual content is written by me (Simon), and all project and music ideas were conceptualized in my own brain. AI can be many things, and for this website I'm choosing to use it as a tool, but not a crutch. Perhaps I'll have to write a short blog post detailing my thoughts on the proper place for automation/AI, but that is a task for another day.

My Favorite Feature So Far

My wife and I love taking polaroid pictures. There's something about the limited nature of physical photos that makes them sweet and nostalgic. I thought it would be a nice touch for my landing page to have a homey, almost scrapbook vibe to it. The pile of polaroids sitting on the desk gets shuffled with each refresh, adding a little variability.

One feature that took a ton of iteration is the animation of the "flick" movement when a user clicks the stack. The top photo quickly fades as it accelerates off the page to the right, after which the stack "settles" into its new position. In order to speed up the performance, I limited the stack to 5 polaroids rendered at a time. Because they blur as they are further back in the pile, this nearly gives the illusion of an endless stack.

I was so excited when I finally got the polaroid stack functioning properly, and it still brings me joy just to sit and click through my random collection of photos over the years. I hope it brings the same to you.

Hosting This Website

I purchased simonarneberg.com domain name in early 2025, but it took me a year to actually get around to making the website. I had some light html/css experience from a web development class in my CS program, but I had to learn a ton in order to get this site up and running on the internet properly. If you're interested in mimicking anything I did, here are some of the steps I had to take:

  1. Write the code for the website (stored in this GitHub repository).
  2. Run locally using Python's built-in http.server to test changes.
  3. Deploy the site via GitHub Pages (free for static sites).
  4. Purchase a custom domain from Name.com
  5. Configure domain to point at GitHub Pages (DNS records and CNAME file).
  6. Whenever any changes are pushed to the main branch, the site updates automatically in less than a minute. Pretty smooth!

The Future of simonarneberg.com

I'm hoping to continually improve this site as I learn more, and as I have more ideas. In an ideal world, I would have enough content on the backburner to start producing a meaningful project blog post each month or week. But we'll see if that consistency is possible. I do have some future projects in mind that I'd like to create, and I'm also open to hear any ideas that you might have!

But for now, peace out.
